THE CLEVER LEVER

After my discussion with Poppin, I wanted to explore the
desktop hook but with a simple latching mechanism. I
tried several options, but most of them were too complex
and exposed for Poppin’s design language. I decided to
model a solution, further using Poppin’s own “clever
lever” latching system.

FEEDBACK TAKE 2

After meeting with Poppin in person, they no longer
felt like the height of the hook was an issue. However,
they did feel that the hardware of the lever was too
visible for Poppin’s brand aesthetic. They offered some
insight for a concealed spring-loaded mechanism.

Overall, Poppin was not concerned with making it
customizable for all desks but rather a decent
range of desk thicknesses. They really appreciated
my original “tree-like” form.

THE SPRING

Back to the drawing board

I tried to find a simple solution for a spring-loaded
mechanism that would provide constant pressure on
the tabletop for support. I created a model to
demonstrate the spring action.

Polystyrene (1/8" Plastic Sheet)

Is the spring too much?

I broke the form into two connecting pieces and
added a spring mechanism for downward table
pressure. Ultimately, even this solution was too
complex. The form could exist simply and could
exclusively fit Poppin’s Series A desk system.
